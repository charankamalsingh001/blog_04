These days, automation is ubiquitous. While economists frequently discuss whether automation generates or eliminates employment, they pay less attention to how tasks are changed and the wages provided to the workers who perform them. Automation occasionally results in higher-paying jobs, but more frequently it lowers wages. This may seem advantageous for businesses thinking about automating because lower labour costs can boost profits.
